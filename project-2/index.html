Brick Animation

Here are some quick tips to help you make a Brick Animation.

Suggested Materials:2


Editing Software Photoshop Adobe Auditon After Effects
Digital Camera Canon DSLR Tripod or Mount Shutter Switch
Legos Characters Bricks Boards
Creative Media Paints Recycled Materials Strangers Objects

Instructions:3



Gather materials.


Build your scene.


Lights, camera, placement.


Mark the placement of all production materials.


Start animating your scene.


Keep the movements small.


Take more photos than necessary.


You can always delete extras, but reshooting from scratch is exspensive.


Use flat colors on objects that will be deleted.


I used green bricks to create this illusion.


Then I took my images to photoshop and erased as much as I could.


I then started to animate on top of photo.


Because smoke is dynamic, it changes drastically in each frame.


In this case, the bigger the better.


Notice how not all of the green bricks were erased.


Luckily that can still be fixed in After Effects or back in Photoshop.


This animation was turned into a GIF.


It might seem like a lot of work at first, but the payoff is more than worth it.


You don't have to limit yourself to Legos either.


Anything can become a stop motion animation.


Be creative.


And always try something new.


If you liked this tutorial, be sure to share this webpage.


You can also find me and more tutorials on YouTube, Instagram and Twitter.


Check back regularly for more updates.


Keep animating.
